# Diátaxis Template: Explanation

**Mode**: Understanding-Oriented
**Purpose**: Build deep conceptual understanding
**User Need**: "I want to understand X"

## Template Structure

### 1. Introduction: What We'll Explore
- **Pattern**: Establish significance and scope
- **Tone**: Thoughtful, inviting inquiry
- **Length**: 2-3 sentences

```markdown
{TOPIC} {SIGNIFICANCE_STATEMENT}. This exploration examines {ASPECT_1}, {ASPECT_2}, and {ASPECT_3}, revealing how {DEEPER_UNDERSTANDING}. Understanding {TOPIC} changes how you approach {PRACTICAL_APPLICATION}.
```

### 2. Context: Historical or Theoretical Foundation
- **Pattern**: Background that explains "why"
- **Tone**: Narrative, contextual
- **Format**: Chronological or logical progression

```markdown
## Historical Context

{CONCEPT} emerged from {ORIGIN_STORY}. {KEY_FIGURE} recognized that {INSIGHT}, leading to {DEVELOPMENT}. This foundation shapes how we {CURRENT_PRACTICE}.
```

or

```markdown
## Theoretical Foundation

{CONCEPT} rests on {THEORETICAL_BASIS}. The core insight: {KEY_PRINCIPLE}. This principle explains why {OBSERVABLE_PHENOMENON}.
```

### 3. Core Concept: Deep Dive
- **Pattern**: Concept + Analogy + Elaboration
- **Tone**: Exploratory, building understanding layer by layer
- **Structure**: Progressive revelation

```markdown
## Understanding {CONCEPT}

{CONCEPT} is {INITIAL_DEFINITION}. Like {ANALOGY}, it {ANALOGY_MAPPING}. But this analogy only captures {PARTIAL_UNDERSTANDING}—the full picture requires examining {DEEPER_ASPECTS}.

{ELABORATION_PARAGRAPH_1}. {ELABORATION_PARAGRAPH_2}. {ELABORATION_PARAGRAPH_3}.

This depth matters because {PRACTICAL_IMPLICATIONS}.
```

### 4. How It Works: Mechanism Explanation
- **Pattern**: Break down into understandable components
- **Tone**: Explanatory, revealing internals
- **Format**: Subsections per component or aspect

```markdown
## How {CONCEPT} Works

### Component 1: {ASPECT_NAME}

{EXPLANATION_OF_ASPECT_1}. {HOW_IT_RELATES_TO_WHOLE}. {WHY_IT_MATTERS}.

**Example**: {CONCRETE_ILLUSTRATION}

### Component 2: {ANOTHER_ASPECT}

{EXPLANATION_OF_ASPECT_2}. {INTERACTION_WITH_ASPECT_1}. {EMERGENT_BEHAVIOR}.

**Analogy**: {EXPLANATORY_ANALOGY}
```

### 5. Why It Matters: Significance
- **Pattern**: Connect concept to broader context
- **Tone**: Reflective, significance-establishing
- **Format**: Thematic paragraphs

```markdown
## Why {CONCEPT} Matters

### Theoretical Significance

{CONCEPT} challenges {PREVIOUS_UNDERSTANDING} by {NEW_INSIGHT}. This shift {THEORETICAL_IMPLICATION}.

### Practical Significance

Understanding {CONCEPT} changes how you {PRACTICAL_ACTIVITY}. Instead of {OLD_APPROACH}, you recognize {BETTER_APPROACH}. This explains why {OBSERVABLE_OUTCOME}.

### Historical Significance

{CONCEPT}'s development enabled {BREAKTHROUGH}. Without it, {WHAT_WOULDNT_EXIST}. Its influence extends beyond {ORIGINAL_DOMAIN} into {RELATED_FIELDS}.
```

### 6. Common Misconceptions: Clarifying Confusion
- **Pattern**: Misconception → Why it's wrong → Correct understanding
- **Tone**: Corrective, patient
- **Format**: Subsections per misconception

```markdown
## Common Misconceptions

### Misconception: "{WRONG_BELIEF}"

Many believe {MISCONCEPTION}, assuming {FAULTY_REASONING}. This misunderstanding arises from {WHY_PEOPLE_THINK_THIS}.

**Reality**: {CORRECT_UNDERSTANDING}. The confusion stems from {ROOT_OF_CONFUSION}. Understanding {KEY_DISTINCTION} resolves this.

**Example**: {ILLUSTRATIVE_CASE}
```

### 7. Connections: Relationships to Other Concepts
- **Pattern**: Map concept to broader landscape
- **Tone**: Integrative, showing relationships
- **Format**: Conceptual map or thematic paragraphs

```markdown
## Connections

### Relationship to {RELATED_CONCEPT_1}

{CONCEPT} and {RELATED_CONCEPT_1} {RELATIONSHIP}. While {CONCEPT} handles {ASPECT_A}, {RELATED_CONCEPT_1} addresses {ASPECT_B}. Together, they {COMBINED_EFFECT}.

### Influence on {RELATED_CONCEPT_2}

{CONCEPT} directly enabled {RELATED_CONCEPT_2} by {ENABLING_MECHANISM}. Without {CONCEPT}, {RELATED_CONCEPT_2} would {LIMITATION}.

### Contrast with {OPPOSING_CONCEPT}

Unlike {OPPOSING_CONCEPT}, which {OPPOSING_APPROACH}, {CONCEPT} {OUR_APPROACH}. This difference matters when {USE_CASE_DISTINCTION}.
```

### 8. Conclusion: Synthesis and Implications
- **Pattern**: Bring insights together, point forward
- **Tone**: Synthesizing, thought-provoking
- **Format**: Reflective paragraph(s)

```markdown
## Conclusion

{CONCEPT} reveals {CORE_INSIGHT}. By understanding {KEY_ASPECTS}, we see how {BROADER_IMPLICATION}. This understanding transforms {PRACTICAL_ACTIVITY} from {MECHANICAL_APPROACH} to {INFORMED_APPROACH}.

The implications extend beyond immediate application. As {TREND_OR_DEVELOPMENT}, {CONCEPT}'s significance grows because {FUTURE_RELEVANCE}. Mastering {CONCEPT} positions you to {FUTURE_OPPORTUNITY}.
```

## Key Principles

### DO: Explanation Best Practices
✅ **Build understanding progressively**: Simple → Complex
✅ **Use analogies liberally**: Make abstract concepts concrete
✅ **Explain "why" not just "what"**: Reveal underlying principles
✅ **Connect to broader context**: Show relationships and significance
✅ **Address misconceptions**: Clarify common confusions
✅ **Be thought-provoking**: Invite deeper reflection
✅ **Support with evidence**: Ground insights in research/data

### DON'T: Explanation Anti-Patterns
❌ **Give instructions**: Don't tell users what to do (use How-To)
❌ **Teach hands-on**: Don't provide step-by-step (use Tutorial)
❌ **Just list facts**: Don't be dry reference material (use Reference)
❌ **Stay shallow**: Don't avoid complexity—explanations go deep
❌ **Ignore history**: Don't present concepts as disconnected from origins
❌ **Assert without evidence**: Don't make claims without research backing

## Topic Sentence Patterns

**Primary Pattern**: Concept + Analogy
- "{CONCEPT} is {DEFINITION}. Like {ANALOGY}, it {MAPPING}."
- "{CONCEPT} {MECHANISM}. This {WHY_IT_MATTERS}."

**Secondary Pattern**: Question + Answer
- "Why does {PHENOMENON}? {EXPLANATION}. {DEEPER_INSIGHT}."
- "What explains {OBSERVATION}? {CONCEPT} provides the answer: {MECHANISM}."

**Tertiary Pattern**: Problem + Solution (for historical context)
- "{HISTORICAL_PROBLEM} plagued {FIELD} until {INNOVATION}. {CONCEPT} resolved this by {MECHANISM}."

**Avoid**: Action + Result (too instructional), Definition + Importance (too reference-like unless building to analogy)

## Example Paragraph

**Good Explanation Paragraph**:
```markdown
Gradient descent finds function minima by following the direction of steepest decrease at each iteration. Like a hiker reading a topographic map to find the steepest downhill path, the algorithm calculates gradients (directional derivatives) that point toward the valley. Each step moves in this direction by a fixed amount (the learning rate), gradually converging on the minimum. This elegant mechanism enabled the deep learning revolution by making neural network training computationally feasible—before gradient descent, training multi-layer networks was intractable because the optimization problem had too many dimensions for exhaustive search.
```

**Analysis**:
- ✅ Concept explained (gradient descent mechanism)
- ✅ Analogy provided (hiker with topo map)
- ✅ Technical detail (gradients, learning rate)
- ✅ Significance established (enabled deep learning)
- ✅ Historical context (what problem it solved)
- ✅ Builds understanding progressively
- ✅ Thought-provoking (connects mechanism to impact)

## Diátaxis Mode Integration

### When to Use Explanation Mode
- **User Intent**: Understanding-seeking, wants to know "why"
- **User State**: Competent enough to engage with complexity
- **Content Goal**: Build conceptual mental models
- **Success Metric**: User can explain concept to others

### Relationship to Other Modes
- **Explanation ← Tutorial**: After hands-on practice, user seeks deeper understanding
- **Explanation → How-To**: Understanding enables user to adapt solutions creatively
- **Explanation ↔ Reference**: User alternates between deep dives and fact lookups

### Evidence Integration in Explanations
- **Essential**: Explanations make claims about how things work—evidence validates
- **Example**: "Mayer's Cognitive Load Theory demonstrates that presenting all information simultaneously overwhelms working memory, which holds only 5-9 chunks (Miller 1956)."
- **Use liberally**: Citations establish authority and grounding in research
- **Pattern**: Concept explanation → Evidence cite → Elaboration

## Variable Placeholders

Replace these during content generation:

- `{TOPIC}`: The concept being explained (e.g., "Type systems")
- `{SIGNIFICANCE_STATEMENT}`: Why it matters (e.g., "fundamentally shape how we write reliable code")
- `{ASPECT_N}`: Facets to explore (e.g., "compile-time guarantees", "inference mechanisms")
- `{DEEPER_UNDERSTANDING}`: Insight gained (e.g., "types prevent entire error categories")
- `{PRACTICAL_APPLICATION}`: Where understanding applies (e.g., "language selection")
- `{ORIGIN_STORY}`: Historical background
- `{KEY_FIGURE}`: Important contributor
- `{INSIGHT}`: Core realization that led to development
- `{THEORETICAL_BASIS}`: Foundational theory
- `{KEY_PRINCIPLE}`: Central insight
- `{CONCEPT}`: Specific concept being explained
- `{INITIAL_DEFINITION}`: Simple starting definition
- `{ANALOGY}`: Concrete comparison
- `{ANALOGY_MAPPING}`: How analogy maps to concept
- `{ELABORATION_PARAGRAPH_N}`: Deeper exploration
- `{PRACTICAL_IMPLICATIONS}`: Why depth matters
- `{ASPECT_NAME}`: Component or facet name
- `{MISCONCEPTION}`: Common wrong belief
- `{FAULTY_REASONING}`: Why people believe misconception
- `{CORRECT_UNDERSTANDING}`: Actual truth
- `{RELATED_CONCEPT_N}`: Connected concept
- `{RELATIONSHIP}`: How concepts relate
- `{OPPOSING_CONCEPT}`: Contrasting approach
- `{CORE_INSIGHT}`: Main takeaway
- `{TREND_OR_DEVELOPMENT}`: Future direction

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing Explanation content, verify:

- [ ] Core concept clearly defined with analogy
- [ ] Historical or theoretical context provided
- [ ] "Why" questions addressed (not just "what")
- [ ] Analogies used to make abstract concepts concrete
- [ ] Evidence cited for claims about mechanisms
- [ ] Common misconceptions addressed
- [ ] Connections to related concepts shown
- [ ] Significance established (theoretical and practical)
- [ ] Progressive complexity (simple → deep)
- [ ] Thought-provoking insights offered
- [ ] No instructions or prescriptions (stays explanatory)
- [ ] Builds mental models (reader can explain concept after)
