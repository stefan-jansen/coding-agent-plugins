# Skill: Anti-Consultant Web Copy

**Purpose**: Write website content that's direct, honest, and strips away consultant-speak. Engineer-to-engineer honesty instead of framework fluff.

**When to use**: Landing pages, service pages, capability descriptions, any B2B technical content where authenticity matters more than sounding sophisticated.

---

## Core Principle

**Instead of making simple things sound complex, make complex things sound simple.**

Clients don't buy frameworks. They buy solutions to specific, painful problems they're experiencing right now.

---

## What to Strip Out

### 1. Buzzwords and Framework-Speak

❌ **Bad**: "Compounding Leverage through our Operating Stack"
✅ **Good**: "Build shared infrastructure so your 10th model doesn't start from scratch"

❌ **Bad**: "Layer 0: Organizational Readiness"
✅ **Good**: "Figure out who owns what (most orgs skip this and models never reach production)"

❌ **Bad**: "Systems That Endure"
✅ **Good**: "Build stuff that doesn't break after 6 months"

❌ **Bad**: "Strategic transformation leveraging AI capabilities"
✅ **Good**: "Help you deploy AI systems that actually work in production"

### 2. Artificial Structure

❌ **Bad**: Numbered layers, phases, stages that create proprietary-sounding methodology
✅ **Good**: Describe the actual sequence: "First figure out who owns it, then build it, then keep it working"

❌ **Bad**: "The Compounding AI Operating Stack consists of four discrete layers..."
✅ **Good**: "Most teams skip the boring org stuff and jump straight to building models. Then they wonder why nothing reaches production."

### 3. Consultant Verbs

**Strip these words:**
- Leverage
- Optimize
- Transform
- Enable
- Empower
- Synergize
- Accelerate (unless you have proof)
- Maximize
- Streamline

**Use these instead:**
- Build
- Fix
- Reduce (with numbers)
- Make cheaper/faster/better (with numbers)
- Prevent
- Figure out
- Help

### 4. Abstract Claims

❌ **Bad**: "Enhance operational efficiency"
✅ **Good**: "Cut the time to deploy a new forecast from 3 weeks to 3 days"

❌ **Bad**: "Drive business value through AI"
✅ **Good**: "Your forecasts stop breaking every time the market shifts"

❌ **Bad**: "Best-in-class methodology"
✅ **Good**: "We've done this 20+ times and here's what works"

---

## What to Include Instead

### 1. Actual Client Pain Points (In Their Words)

**Examples:**
- "Our models work great in testing then degrade after 6 months in production"
- "Each team builds their own feature engineering and we're duplicating everything"
- "We have 12 pilots but can't get anything to production"
- "Governance blocks every deployment for months"
- "We keep rebuilding from scratch because we can't maintain what we built"

**Pattern**: [Problem they're experiencing] → [Why it matters] → [What you do about it]

### 2. Concrete Numbers (Even if Anonymized)

❌ **Bad**: "Significant reduction in deployment time"
✅ **Good**: "Deployment from 6 months to 3-6 weeks"

❌ **Bad**: "Improved model performance"
✅ **Good**: "88% precision maintained over 3 years despite market shifts"

❌ **Bad**: "Reduced duplication"
✅ **Good**: "75% reduction in duplicated feature engineering"

**If you don't have numbers, don't make vague claims**

### 3. Honest Tradeoffs

**Always include "when NOT to use this":**

"This is overkill if you're only running 2-3 models. The overhead isn't worth it until you hit 5+ models and start feeling the duplication pain."

"This takes 3-6 months of upfront work before you ship anything. If you need demos next quarter, this isn't the right approach."

"You need exec buy-in and cross-team coordination. If you don't have that, build one-off systems instead."

### 4. Straight Talk About What You Do

❌ **Bad**: "We architect transformative AI solutions leveraging cutting-edge methodologies"
✅ **Good**: "We build production ML infrastructure and help you organize teams so your AI systems don't fall apart"

❌ **Bad**: "Our proprietary framework enables systematic value capture"
✅ **Good**: "We've seen the same problems 20 times. Here's what works and what doesn't."

## Structure for Service/Capability Pages

### Opening (2-3 sentences)
**Bad pattern**: Framework introduction
**Good pattern**: State the problem directly

❌ "Layer 1 of our Operating Stack provides the substrate for..."
✅ "Most teams treat infrastructure as afterthought. Every model needs custom pipelines, manual testing, bespoke monitoring. You hit a hard ceiling at 3-5 models."

### What We Do (2-3 paragraphs)
**Bad pattern**: Abstract capabilities list
**Good pattern**: Specific problems → Specific solutions → Specific outcomes

"We build shared infrastructure:
- Centralized feature stores (stop rebuilding the same features)
- Standardized evaluation (apples-to-apples comparison)
- Automated deployment pipelines (stop manual copying to production servers)

Result: 10th model takes 3 days instead of 3 weeks. First model is slower (building infrastructure), but you make it back by model #3."

### Examples (3-4 bullets)
**Bad pattern**: Generic industry + vague outcome
**Good pattern**: Specific problem + Specific solution + Specific metric

❌ "Fortune 500 Financial Services: Enhanced forecasting capabilities"
✅ "Healthcare Payer: 50+ forecasts, each taking 3-4 weeks to update. Built shared feature store and evaluation harness. Next 10 forecasts took 3-5 days each (60-70% reduction)."

### When NOT to Use (1-2 sentences)
**Critical**: Always include this

"Overkill for <5 models. Requires 3-6 months upfront investment. Feels slow until you're maintaining multiple systems."

## Tone Markers

### ✅ Sounds Right
- "Here's what actually works"
- "Most teams skip this and wonder why..."
- "This is boring org stuff but it's why pilots never scale"
- "You need X. If you don't have X, do Y instead."
- "This takes 3 months before you ship anything"
- "The 10th model is cheaper than the first"

### ❌ Sounds Like Consultant-Speak
- "Our methodology leverages..."
- "Best-in-class approach"
- "Strategic transformation"
- "Paradigm shift"
- "Cutting-edge solution"
- "Proven framework"
- "Optimize business outcomes"

---

## Litmus Test

**Before publishing, ask:**

1. **Would an engineer read this and think "yeah, that's the actual problem"?**
   - If no: Too abstract, add specifics

2. **Does it sound like you're selling a framework or solving a problem?**
   - If framework: Strip the structure, talk about problems

3. **Can you defend every claim with either data or "we've done this 20+ times"?**
   - If no: Remove the claim

4. **Would you be embarrassed to say this out loud to a technical peer?**
   - If yes: It's consultant-speak, rewrite

5. **Does it include honest tradeoffs and "when NOT to use"?**
   - If no: Add them

## Examples: Before/After

### Example 1: Service Description

❌ **Before (Consultant-Speak)**:
"Our Layer 1 capabilities enable organizations to architect scalable AI infrastructure through our proprietary Compounding Operating Stack, leveraging shared substrates and reusable patterns to optimize deployment velocity while maintaining governance excellence."

✅ **After (Direct)**:
"We build shared ML infrastructure so your 10th model doesn't start from scratch. Centralized feature engineering, standardized testing, automated deployment. First model takes 6 weeks (building the platform), next 10 models take 3-5 days each. Overkill if you're only running 2-3 models."

### Example 2: Problem Statement

❌ **Before**:
"Organizations face challenges in operationalizing AI capabilities at scale, struggling with siloed development patterns and lack of systematic governance frameworks."

✅ **After**:
"Every team builds their own feature engineering. Your 5th model is rebuilding what model 2 already did. Governance blocks every deployment for months because there's no consistent approval path."

### Example 3: Capability Description

❌ **Before**:
"Layer 0: Organizational Readiness provides the foundational substrate enabling systematic AI transformation through clearly defined operating models and governance frameworks."

✅ **After**:
"Figure out who owns what before building anything. Most teams skip this—build technically brilliant models with no one to maintain them. We help define ownership (Model Owner, Data Steward, Business Sponsor) and decision rights (who decides when to retrain vs rollback). Takes 3-6 months but prevents pilot purgatory."

---

## Summary: The Shift

**From**: Making simple things sound complex to justify consulting fees
**To**: Making complex things sound simple because we actually understand them

**From**: Proprietary frameworks and methodology™
**To**: "We've done this 20+ times, here's what works"

**From**: Abstract value propositions
**To**: Specific problems, specific solutions, specific numbers

**From**: Consultant verbs (leverage, optimize, transform)
**To**: Engineer verbs (build, fix, prevent, reduce)

**From**: Hiding tradeoffs
**To**: Honest about when NOT to use

---

**Remember**: If you wouldn't say it to an engineering peer over coffee, don't put it on the website.
