# SEO Copywriting

## Overview

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) copywriting is the practice of creating content that ranks well in search engines while remaining compelling and valuable to human readers. This skill focuses on technical SEO writing techniques for web content.

**When to use**: Website pages, landing pages, service descriptions, blog posts intended for organic search traffic.

**Version**: v1.0 (Added in writing-skills v1.1.0)

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Search Intent First

Write for the user's search intent, not just keywords:
- **Informational intent**: "How to...", "What is...", "Guide to..."
- **Transactional intent**: "Buy...", "Consulting services for...", "Hire..."
- **Navigational intent**: Brand searches, specific page lookups

Match your content structure and tone to the dominant intent.

### 2. Keywords as Natural Language

**Don't**: Stuff keywords unnaturally ("AI consulting AI experts AI services")
**Do**: Use keywords naturally in context ("Our AI consulting experts provide strategic services...")

**Primary keyword**: Use 1-2 times in first 100 words
**Related keywords**: Sprinkle semantically related terms throughout
**Long-tail keywords**: Target 3-5 word phrases with specific intent

### 3. E-E-A-T: Expertise, Experience, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness

Google evaluates content quality through E-E-A-T signals:
- **Expertise**: Demonstrate domain knowledge through depth and accuracy
- **Experience**: Show first-hand experience (case examples, lessons learned)
- **Authoritativeness**: Cite credentials, publications, recognized expertise
- **Trustworthiness**: Transparent, honest, well-sourced content

## Technical SEO Elements

### Title Tags (50-60 characters)

**Purpose**: Appears in search results, critical for click-through rate

**Best practices**:
- Include primary keyword near the beginning
- Make it compelling and click-worthy
- Include brand name (if space allows)
- Stay under 60 characters to avoid truncation

**Example**:
```
Bad:  "Services We Offer at Our Consulting Firm"
Good: "AI & ML Consulting Services | Predictive Analytics Experts"
```

### Meta Descriptions (150-160 characters)

**Purpose**: Appears below title in search results, influences click-through

**Best practices**:
- Summarize page value proposition
- Include primary keyword naturally
- Include call-to-action if appropriate
- Stay under 160 characters
- Make it compelling and benefit-focused

**Example**:
```
Bad:  "We are a consulting firm that does AI and machine learning."
Good: "Expert AI/ML consulting for Fortune 500 firms. Predictive analytics, LLMs, and algorithmic trading. 13+ years experience. Contact us today."
```

### Heading Hierarchy (H1, H2, H3)

**Purpose**: Organizes content for readers and search engines

**Best practices**:
- **H1**: One per page, includes primary keyword, describes page topic
- **H2**: Major sections, include related keywords or variations
- **H3**: Subsections under H2s, support the hierarchy
- Use headings to create scannable content structure

**Example structure**:
```
H1: Predictive Analytics Consulting for Customer Retention
  H2: Why Customer Churn Prediction Matters
    H3: Financial Impact of Retention
    H3: Competitive Advantages
  H2: Our Uplift Modeling Approach
    H3: Feature Engineering for Behavioral Data
    H3: Model Evaluation and Deployment
  H2: Industries We Serve
```

### URL Structure

**Purpose**: Clean, descriptive URLs improve usability and SEO

**Best practices**:
- Keep URLs short and readable
- Use hyphens to separate words
- Include primary keyword
- Avoid parameters and IDs when possible

**Example**:
```
Bad:  /services?id=123&cat=ai
Good: /services/predictive-analytics-consulting
```

## Content Structure for SEO

### Opening Paragraph (First 100 words)

**Critical for SEO**: Search engines weight early content heavily

**Include**:
- Primary keyword (naturally)
- Clear value proposition
- Answer to search intent
- Hook to keep reader engaged

**Example**:
```
Applied AI provides expert machine learning consulting for Fortune 500
companies navigating complex predictive analytics challenges. With 13+
years of experience deploying models in production—from pharmaceutical
benefit management to algorithmic trading—we transform business problems
into scalable AI solutions. Our approach emphasizes practical deployment,
long-term model governance, and measurable ROI.
```

### Body Content Best Practices

1. **Use bullets and numbered lists**: Improves scannability, may trigger featured snippets
2. **Answer questions directly**: Target "People Also Ask" opportunities
3. **Include semantic keywords**: Related terms and synonyms naturally
4. **Add internal links**: Link to related service pages and content
5. **Use descriptive anchor text**: "Learn more about uplift modeling" vs "click here"

### Content Length Guidelines

- **Landing pages**: 300-500 words (concise, compelling)
- **Service pages**: 500-1,000 words (comprehensive, authoritative)
- **Blog posts**: 1,500-2,500 words (deep dives, thought leadership)
- **Cornerstone content**: 2,500+ words (definitive guides, updated regularly)

Quality beats quantity—don't add fluff to hit word counts.

## Long-Tail Keyword Strategy

### What Are Long-Tail Keywords?

3-5 word phrases with:
- Lower search volume
- Lower competition
- Higher conversion intent
- More specific user needs

### Examples for AI/ML Consulting

**Generic (high competition)**:
- "AI consulting"
- "machine learning services"

**Long-tail (targeted, valuable)**:
- "uplift modeling retention marketing consulting"
- "document intelligence LLM insurance contracts"
- "algorithmic trading machine learning deployment"
- "predictive analytics pharmaceutical benefit management"

### How to Use Long-Tail Keywords

1. **Target in H2/H3 headings**: Structure content around specific queries
2. **Answer specific questions**: "How to build uplift models for retention"
3. **Industry + service combinations**: "AI consulting for pharmaceutical firms"
4. **Problem-oriented queries**: "Reducing customer churn with predictive analytics"

## Image Optimization

### Alt Text

**Purpose**: Accessibility and image search SEO

**Best practices**:
- Describe image content accurately
- Include keyword if naturally relevant
- Keep under 125 characters
- Write for blind users first, search engines second

**Example**:
```
Bad:  alt="image1"
Good: alt="Uplift modeling workflow diagram showing feature engineering and evaluation steps"
```

### File Names

Use descriptive, keyword-rich file names:
```
Bad:  IMG_20241101.jpg
Good: predictive-analytics-customer-retention-chart.jpg
```

## Internal Linking Strategy

### Purpose

- Helps users discover related content
- Distributes page authority across site
- Helps search engines understand site structure
- Increases time-on-site

### Best Practices

1. **Link from service pages to insights**: "Read our guide to uplift modeling"
2. **Link from insights to services**: "Need help deploying these models? See our predictive analytics consulting"
3. **Use descriptive anchor text**: Include keywords naturally
4. **Link to cornerstone content**: Major pages should receive many internal links
5. **Avoid excessive linking**: 2-5 contextual links per page is sufficient

## Schema Markup (Structured Data)

### What It Is

Machine-readable markup that helps search engines understand content type and show rich snippets.

### Common Schema Types for Consulting

- **Organization**: Company info, logo, social profiles
- **Service**: Individual service offerings
- **Article**: Blog posts and insights
- **Person**: Author bios and credentials
- **BreadcrumbList**: Navigation hierarchy

### Benefits

- Enhanced search result displays (rich snippets)
- Better content understanding by search engines
- Potential for featured snippets
- Improved click-through rates

**Note**: Schema implementation is technical (JSON-LD in HTML), often handled by developers.

## Measuring SEO Success

### Key Metrics

1. **Organic traffic**: Visitors from search engines
2. **Keyword rankings**: Position for target keywords
3. **Click-through rate (CTR)**: Impressions vs clicks in search results
4. **Time on page**: Engagement indicator
5. **Bounce rate**: Do visitors find what they need?
6. **Conversions**: Leads, contacts, downloads from organic traffic

### Tools

- **Google Search Console**: Track performance, impressions, clicks, rankings
- **Google Analytics**: Traffic sources, behavior, conversions
- **Keyword research tools**: Identify opportunities and competition

## Common SEO Copywriting Mistakes

### ❌ Avoid These

1. **Keyword stuffing**: Unnatural repetition of keywords
2. **Thin content**: Pages with <300 words, little value
3. **Duplicate content**: Same content on multiple pages
4. **Hidden text**: White text on white background, etc.
5. **Over-optimization**: Every sentence has keywords
6. **Ignoring user intent**: Writing for search engines, not humans
7. **No internal links**: Isolated pages with no connections
8. **Generic meta descriptions**: "Welcome to our website..."
9. **Poor mobile experience**: Not mobile-friendly or readable

### ✅ Do This Instead

1. **Write naturally**: Keywords in context, conversational tone
2. **Provide value**: Answer questions, solve problems, share expertise
3. **Unique perspectives**: Add information gain, don't repeat competitors
4. **Match intent**: Understand what users want when they search
5. **Update regularly**: Keep content current, especially technical topics
6. **Focus on E-E-A-T**: Demonstrate expertise through quality and depth
7. **Test and iterate**: Monitor performance, refine based on data

## SEO Copywriting Checklist

### Before Writing

- [ ] Identify primary keyword and 2-3 related keywords
- [ ] Research search intent for target keyword
- [ ] Analyze top-ranking competitor content
- [ ] Determine appropriate content length

### During Writing

- [ ] Include primary keyword in first 100 words naturally
- [ ] Use keyword in H1 and 1-2 H2 headings
- [ ] Structure with clear heading hierarchy
- [ ] Add bullets/lists for scannability
- [ ] Include 2-5 internal links with descriptive anchor text
- [ ] Write compelling, benefit-focused content
- [ ] Answer user's search intent directly

### After Writing

- [ ] Craft title tag (50-60 chars, includes keyword)
- [ ] Write meta description (150-160 chars, compelling)
- [ ] Optimize images (alt text, file names)
- [ ] Check URL structure (clean, includes keyword)
- [ ] Proofread for quality and clarity
- [ ] Verify mobile-friendly formatting
- [ ] Add schema markup (if applicable)

## Example: SEO-Optimized Service Page

### Title Tag
```
Predictive Analytics Consulting | Customer Retention Models | Applied AI
```

### Meta Description
```
Expert predictive analytics consulting for Fortune 500 firms. Uplift modeling, churn prediction, and lifecycle forecasting. 13+ years production experience.
```

### H1
```
Predictive Analytics Consulting for Customer Retention
```

```
Applied AI's predictive analytics consulting helps enterprises reduce
customer churn and maximize retention ROI through advanced machine
learning. Our team has deployed uplift models, next-best-action systems,
and lifecycle forecasting solutions for Fortune 500 clients across
pharmaceutical, insurance, and financial services industries.

  H3: Pharmaceutical Benefit Management
  H3: Insurance Retention Programs
  H3: Financial Services Cross-Sell
H2: How We Work
H2: Get Started
```
