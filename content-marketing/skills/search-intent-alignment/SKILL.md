# Search Intent Alignment

## Overview

Search intent alignment is the practice of matching your content to what users actually want when they search. Understanding and satisfying search intent is critical for SEO success and user satisfaction—if your content doesn't match what users seek, they'll bounce quickly, harming both conversions and rankings.

**When to use**: Website pages, blog posts, landing pages, any content intended to rank in search engines.

**Version**: v1.0 (Added in writing-skills v1.1.0)

---

## What Is Search Intent?

**Search intent** (or "user intent") is the goal behind a search query. When someone types "machine learning consulting" or "how to reduce customer churn," they have a specific need. Your content must satisfy that need to succeed.

**Why it matters**:
- Google explicitly ranks content by how well it satisfies intent
- Mismatched intent = high bounce rate = poor rankings
- Matched intent = engaged users = conversions

**The modern SEO reality**: Keywords alone are insufficient. Intent is king.

## The Four Types of Search Intent

### 1. Informational Intent

**Goal**: Learn, understand, research

**Query patterns**:
- "What is [topic]?"
- "How does [process] work?"
- "Guide to [topic]"
- "Why [phenomenon]?"
- "[Topic] explained"

**Examples**:
- "What is uplift modeling?"
- "How do large language models work?"
- "Guide to customer churn prediction"
- "Why do ML models drift?"

**User expectation**: Educational content, clear explanations, how-to guides, conceptual overviews

**Content approach**:
- Comprehensive explanations
- Step-by-step guides
- Examples and diagrams
- FAQs and definitions
- No hard selling

**Conversion path**: Build trust → capture email → nurture → eventual sale

---

### 2. Navigational Intent

**Goal**: Find a specific website, page, or brand

**Query patterns**:
- "[Brand name]"
- "[Brand name] + [service]"
- "[Brand name] login"
- "[Person name]"

**Examples**:
- "Applied AI consulting"
- "Stefan Jansen machine learning"
- "Applied AI blog"
- "GitHub Claude Code"

**User expectation**: Direct access to the specific entity they're seeking

**Content approach**:
- Clear brand pages with accurate info
- Easy navigation to subpages
- Prominent contact information
- Social media links

**SEO strategy**: Own your brand terms, ensure homepage/key pages rank #1

---

### 3. Transactional Intent

**Goal**: Complete an action, make a purchase, hire a service

**Query patterns**:
- "Buy [product]"
- "Hire [service provider]"
- "[Service] near me"
- "[Service] pricing"
- "Best [category]"
- "[Service] consultant"

**Examples**:
- "Hire machine learning consultant"
- "AI consulting services pricing"
- "Best predictive analytics firm"
- "ML consulting New York"

**User expectation**: Service descriptions, pricing info, credentials, clear path to engagement

**Content approach**:
- Service pages with clear benefits
- Pricing transparency (if possible)
- Credentials and case studies
- Strong calls-to-action
- Contact forms and booking

**Conversion path**: Direct—these users are ready to engage

---

### 4. Commercial Investigation Intent

**Goal**: Research before making a decision, compare options

**Query patterns**:
- "[Product/service] review"
- "[Product A] vs [Product B]"
- "Best [category] for [use case]"
- "[Service] comparison"
- "Top [providers]"

**Examples**:
- "Best AI consulting firms for Fortune 500"
- "Machine learning vs traditional analytics"
- "LLM platforms comparison"
- "How to choose ML consultant"

**User expectation**: Comparisons, reviews, pros/cons, decision frameworks

**Content approach**:
- Honest comparisons
- Buying guides
- Feature matrices
- "How to choose" articles
- Case study highlights

**Conversion path**: Build authority → capture consideration → nurture → convert

## How to Identify Search Intent

### Method 1: Analyze the SERP (Search Engine Results Page)

**The SERP reveals intent**: Google shows what it thinks satisfies the query.

**Process**:
1. Search your target keyword
2. Examine top 10 results
3. Identify patterns

**Ask**:
- Are results mostly blog posts (informational) or service pages (transactional)?
- Are there featured snippets with definitions (informational)?
- Are there "People Also Ask" questions?
- Are there ads (indicates transactional/commercial intent)?
- Are results comparison articles (commercial investigation)?

**Example**: "Customer churn prediction"

```
SERP shows:
- 3 blog posts explaining concepts → Informational dominant
- 2 how-to guides → Informational
- 1 service page → Some transactional
- Featured snippet with definition → Informational

Conclusion: Primarily informational intent, comprehensive guide needed
```

### Method 2: Examine Query Modifiers

**Modifiers signal intent**:

| Modifier | Intent Type | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| "What", "How", "Why" | Informational | "How to reduce churn" |
| "Best", "Top", "Review" | Commercial Investigation | "Best ML consulting firms" |
| "Buy", "Hire", "Pricing" | Transactional | "Hire AI consultant" |
| Brand name | Navigational | "Applied AI consulting" |
| "Near me", "in [city]" | Transactional (local) | "AI consulting New York" |
| "[X] vs [Y]" | Commercial Investigation | "XGBoost vs neural networks" |
| "Guide", "Tutorial" | Informational | "Guide to uplift modeling" |

### Method 3: Consider User Journey Stage

**Intent often maps to buyer journey**:

```
Awareness → Informational intent
  ("What is customer churn?")

Consideration → Commercial Investigation intent
  ("Best retention strategies" / "Churn prediction approaches")

Decision → Transactional intent
  ("Hire retention consultant" / "Customer analytics services")
```

**Match content to stage**: Don't pitch services when users are learning concepts.

## Aligning Content to Intent

### Informational Intent Content Strategy

**Goal**: Educate and build trust

**Content types**:
- How-to guides and tutorials
- Explainer articles
- Concept overviews
- FAQs and glossaries
- Process documentation

**Structure**:
```
H1: [Concept] Explained / How to [Task] / Guide to [Topic]

Introduction: What it is, why it matters (100 words)

H2: Core Concept/Process
  - Clear explanations
  - Examples
  - Visuals/diagrams

H2: Common Questions or Challenges

H2: Next Steps (soft CTA)
  - Link to related content
  - Optional: "Need help implementing? Contact us"
```

**Tone**: Educational, neutral, helpful

**CTAs**: Soft (download guide, read related article, subscribe)

**Example**: "What Is Uplift Modeling? A Complete Guide"

---

### Navigational Intent Content Strategy

**Goal**: Be findable for your brand/name queries

**Content types**:
- Homepage with clear brand positioning
- About pages with credentials
- Service overview pages
- Author/expert bio pages

**Structure**:
```
H1: [Brand Name] – [One-line Positioning]

Hero section: What you do, who you serve (50 words)

H2: Services/Offerings (brief overview)

H2: Why [Brand] (unique differentiators)

H2: Credentials/Experience

CTA: Contact or Explore Services
```

**Tone**: Professional, clear, credible

**CTAs**: Strong (schedule consultation, contact us)

**SEO**: Ensure these pages rank #1 for your brand terms

---

### Transactional Intent Content Strategy

**Goal**: Convert users ready to engage

**Content types**:
- Service pages
- Pricing pages
- Landing pages for specific services
- "Hire [X]" or "Get Started" pages

**Structure**:
```
H1: [Service Name] – [Key Benefit]

Opening: What it is + who it's for + core benefit (30-50 words)

H2: Why [Service]?
  - Problem you solve
  - Approach/methodology
  - Unique differentiators

H2: How It Works
  - Process (3-5 steps)
  - Timeline
  - Deliverables

H2: Who We've Helped
  - Industry examples
  - Results (if shareable)

H2: Pricing/Investment (if appropriate)

H2: Get Started
  - Clear CTA
  - Contact form or calendar
  - Trust signals (credentials, testimonials)
```

**Tone**: Confident, benefit-focused, action-oriented

**CTAs**: Direct and prominent (Schedule Consultation, Contact Us, Get Quote)

**Optimization**: Clear value prop, social proof, low-friction contact

---

### Commercial Investigation Intent Content Strategy

**Goal**: Help users compare and decide

**Content types**:
- "How to choose [service provider]" guides
- "[Approach A] vs [Approach B]" comparisons
- "Best [category] for [use case]" articles
- Vendor comparison pages

**Structure**:
```
H1: How to Choose [Category] / [A] vs [B] / Best [Category] for [Use Case]

Introduction: Why this decision matters (50 words)

H2: Key Factors to Consider
  - Criteria for evaluation
  - What to look for
  - Red flags to avoid

H2: Options/Approaches Comparison
  - Objective comparison (even if it includes competitors)
  - Pros and cons
  - Use case fit

H2: Our Recommendation/Approach
  - Your POV or methodology
  - Why it works
  - Who it's best for

H2: Next Steps
  - CTA: "Need help deciding? Talk to our team"
```

**Tone**: Objective, helpful, authoritative

**CTAs**: Medium strength (consultation, guide download, contact)

**Trust signal**: Honest, balanced analysis (don't bash competitors)

## Mixed Intent Queries

### What Are Mixed Intent Queries?

Some queries satisfy multiple intents simultaneously.

**Example**: "Machine learning consulting"

- **Informational**: "What is ML consulting?" (some users)
- **Navigational**: "Find ML consulting firms" (some users)
- **Transactional**: "Hire ML consultant" (some users)

### How to Handle Mixed Intent

**Strategy 1: Dominant intent primary, secondary intent addressed**

Examine SERP to identify dominant intent, structure page accordingly, address secondary intents with sections or links.

**Example**: "Machine learning consulting"

```
SERP shows mostly service pages (transactional dominant) with some
definition content (informational secondary).

Page structure:
- Primary: Service page (transactional)
- Secondary: Brief "What is ML consulting?" section (informational)
- Links: "Learn more about ML approaches" (informational content on separate page)
```

**Strategy 2: Hub page with pathways**

Create a hub page that segments users by intent.

**Example**: "Customer churn prediction"

```
H1: Customer Churn Prediction

Opening: Brief overview (50 words)

Segment 1: [Informational]
  "New to churn prediction? → Read our complete guide"

Segment 2: [Commercial Investigation]
  "Evaluating approaches? → Compare churn prediction methods"

Segment 3: [Transactional]
  "Ready to implement? → Explore our retention analytics services"
```

## Common Intent Misalignment Mistakes

### ❌ Mistake 1: Selling on Informational Queries

**Problem**: User wants to learn, you pitch services immediately

**Example**:
```
Query: "How to reduce customer churn"
Bad content: Service page selling retention consulting with no educational value
Result: Bounce, no ranking
```

**Fix**: Create comprehensive how-to guide, soft CTA at end

---

### ❌ Mistake 2: Over-Educating on Transactional Queries

**Problem**: User wants to hire, you give long explainer

**Example**:
```
Query: "Hire machine learning consultant NYC"
Bad content: 2,000-word article about what ML is
Result: User leaves to find actual consultant
```

**Fix**: Service page with clear credentials, process, and CTA

---

### ❌ Mistake 3: Ignoring Navigational Intent

**Problem**: Users search your brand, can't find your pages

**Example**:
```
Query: "Applied AI machine learning"
Bad: Competitor or directory ranks above you
Result: Lost brand traffic
```

**Fix**: Optimize homepage and key pages for brand terms

---

### ❌ Mistake 4: Thin Content for Informational Queries

**Problem**: Users want comprehensive info, you give 200-word summary

**Example**:
```
Query: "Uplift modeling guide"
Bad content: Brief definition, vague examples, no depth
Result: Google ranks competitors with comprehensive guides
```

**Fix**: Create substantial, valuable content (1,500-2,500 words)

## Intent-Based Keyword Strategy

### Building Your Keyword-Intent Map

**Process**:
1. List all target keywords
2. Identify intent for each
3. Map to appropriate content type
4. Plan content accordingly

**Example for AI Consulting Firm**:

| Keyword | Intent | Content Type | Priority |
|---------|--------|--------------|----------|
| "What is uplift modeling" | Informational | Blog post/guide | Medium |
| "How to reduce churn" | Informational | How-to article | High |
| "AI consulting services" | Transactional | Service page | High |
| "Best AI consulting firms" | Commercial Inv. | Comparison/positioning | Medium |
| "Applied AI consulting" | Navigational | Homepage | Critical |
| "Hire ML consultant" | Transactional | Landing page | High |
| "XGBoost vs neural networks" | Commercial Inv. | Comparison article | Low |
| "Predictive analytics ROI" | Informational | Case study/guide | Medium |

### Intent-Based Content Calendar

**Plan content across all intent types**:

```
Month 1:
- 2 informational posts (build authority, attract top-of-funnel)
- 1 service page (capture transactional)
- 1 commercial investigation piece (nurture consideration)

Month 2:
- 3 informational posts
- 1 service page enhancement
- 1 comparison article

Continue rotation...
```

**Balance**: 60% informational, 25% transactional, 15% commercial investigation

## Measuring Intent Alignment

### Key Metrics by Intent Type

**Informational intent**:
- Time on page (longer = better)
- Scroll depth (full page read?)
- Internal link clicks (exploring related content?)
- Return visits (found it valuable?)

**Transactional intent**:
- Conversion rate (form submits, calls, bookings)
- Bounce rate (low = good match)
- CTA click-through rate

**Commercial investigation intent**:
- Time on page (researching?)
- Pages per session (comparing options?)
- Email captures (nurturing?)

**Navigational intent**:
- Bounce rate (found what they need = may bounce after confirmation)
- Click to contact info
- Internal navigation to key pages

### Using Google Search Console

**Check intent alignment**:
1. Identify pages and their ranking queries
2. Check average position vs click-through rate
3. High position + low CTR = intent mismatch

**Example**:
```
Page: Service page for "ML consulting"
Query: "What is ML consulting" (informational)
Position: #5
CTR: 1.2%

Diagnosis: Intent mismatch (informational query, transactional page)
Fix: Create separate informational page, link to service page
```

## Intent Evolution Over Time

### Search Intent Can Change

**Early market stage** (concept new):
- Mostly informational queries ("What is generative AI?")
- Focus: Educational content

**Growth stage** (concept maturing):
- Mix of informational + commercial investigation
- "How to choose", "Best approaches", "Comparison" queries increase
- Focus: Thought leadership + differentiation

**Mature stage** (concept established):
- Shift to transactional + commercial investigation
- Direct service/vendor queries dominate
- Focus: Service pages + case studies

**Example**: "Large language models"
- 2020: "What are LLMs?" (informational dominant)
- 2023: "Best LLM platforms" (commercial investigation growing)
- 2024: "Hire LLM consultant" (transactional emerging)

**Strategy**: Monitor query trends, adjust content mix

### Combines Well With

- **seo-copywriting**: Intent alignment determines which SEO tactics to use

### When to Prioritize

**High priority**:
- All web content intended to rank in search
- Landing pages and service pages
- Blog and thought leadership content

**Lower priority**:
- Internal documentation
- Proposal writing
- Email communication

## Intent Alignment Checklist

### Before Creating Content

- [ ] Identify target keyword(s)
- [ ] Search keyword, examine top 10 results
- [ ] Identify dominant intent type
- [ ] Check for mixed intent (secondary needs)
- [ ] Map to appropriate content type
- [ ] Define success metrics for intent type

### While Creating Content

- [ ] Match content format to intent (guide vs service page vs comparison)
- [ ] Use appropriate tone (educational vs sales-focused)
- [ ] Structure content for intent (comprehensive vs concise)
- [ ] Include appropriate CTAs (soft vs direct)
- [ ] Address secondary intents if present

### After Publishing

- [ ] Monitor Google Search Console for ranking queries
- [ ] Check if ranking queries match intended target
- [ ] Measure success metrics for intent type
- [ ] Refine content if intent mismatch detected

## Example: Intent-Aligned Content Strategy

### Scenario: AI Consulting Firm Targeting "Customer Churn"

**Keyword research reveals**:
- "Customer churn prediction" (informational + commercial investigation)
- "How to reduce customer churn" (informational)
- "Churn prediction models" (informational + commercial investigation)
- "Customer retention consulting" (transactional)
- "Hire churn prediction consultant" (transactional)

**Content strategy**:

1. **Informational hub**: "Customer Churn Prediction: Complete Guide"
   - 2,500-word comprehensive guide
   - Targets: "customer churn prediction", "how to reduce churn"
   - Intent: Informational (build authority, capture top-of-funnel)
   - CTA: Soft (download checklist, read related articles)

2. **Commercial investigation**: "Customer Churn Prediction Models: Which Approach Is Right for You?"
   - 1,500-word comparison article
   - Targets: "churn prediction models", "[model A] vs [model B]"
   - Intent: Commercial investigation (help decision-making)
   - CTA: Medium ("Need help choosing? Talk to our team")

3. **Transactional service page**: "Customer Retention Consulting"
   - 600-word service page
   - Targets: "customer retention consulting", "hire churn prediction consultant"
   - Intent: Transactional (convert ready buyers)
   - CTA: Direct (Schedule Consultation, Contact Us)

**Internal linking**:
- Guide → links to comparison article and service page
- Comparison article → links to guide (for basics) and service page (for help)
- Service page → links to guide (for those needing more info)

**Result**: Content satisfies all intents, ranks well, converts across funnel stages
